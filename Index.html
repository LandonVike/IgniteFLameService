Hot Water Heater Repair

Hot Water Tank Replacement

Heating water accounts for up to 30% of the average home’s energy budget. Therefore, it is extremely important to have a system that is running efficiently to minimize your costs. A tankless water heater is usually more energy efficient than a storage tank water heater, since it eliminates the continuous standby energy loss from a storage tank making them a great option for remote locations such as work shops or cottages. These systems can be quite efficient overall, but only optimal when installed and used correctly to provide you on-demand hot water and reduce your energy bills.

Installing a hot water tank isn’t rocket science, but there’s an art to it: to ensure proper drainage, safe water heater pressure, and venting of combustion byproducts, a qualified Ignite Flame Technician knows best. Entrusting the job to a reputable and licensed installer will also pull the required municipal gas permit and call for an inspection is the best way to guarantee a job well done.

A hot water tank (also known as a hot water heater) is suitable for average-use demands for 8-10 years. To avoid mould, mildew, costly renovations, and sky-high insurance premiums—or a flooded basement as a result of a leaking hot water tank—homeowners should begin budgeting for a replacement tank after it has been in service for about eight years.

There are many signs that your hot water tank may need replacing including its age, if it is rumbling when operating or making noise, if you can see leaks, or if you are running out of hot water! If your showers are cold before you are done, you likely need a replacement. A visit from one of our qualified technicians to inspect your hot water tank will also help determine if a replacement is required.

Heat Pump & Air Conditioning Experts

Ignite Flame Services expert HVAC technicians will give your heat pump the care that it needs. Trust us to fix any problem your heat pump may have!

Unlike a traditional heating system an air source heat pump, normally powered by electricity, is like having an air conditioner that can also run in reverse in cooler winter months to provide efficient heating for your home.

Heat pumps provide the option of “dual-fuel” switching from natural gas to electricity (depending on what’s most efficient) saving you money on your gas and electric bills. Despite popular opinion, a heat pump and furnace go hand in hand: The furnace fan pushes air through the system in either the heating or cooling mode. On the coldest days – a rarity with BC’s mild climate – the furnace’s heating cycle automatically takes over to provide the back-up heat when needed.

How should I prepare my furnace for the upcoming cold season?

Preparing your furnace for the fall and winter season in advance can help avoid any potential issues and disruptions in service when the cold comes in. Spend time over the next few weeks to ensure your furnace is running smoothly. Below are some tips from our experts at Ignite Flame Services to prepare your furnace for the cooler months.

  1. Change the Air Filters
    One of the first steps… Dirty air filters can restrict airflow and put unnecessary strain on your furnace, leading to higher energy bills and potential repair issues.
  2. Clean the Furnace and Air Ducts
    Another step… By conducting annual cleanings of your air ducts you can ensure that hot air can effectively reach the entire home.
  3. Test the Furnace and Ignition Switch
    Once you’ve changed… If you have any issues starting the furnace, it is best to seek professional help.
  4. Schedule a Maintenance Check
    It’s also a good idea to schedule a furnace tune-up with a qualified technician before winter.

Following these tips will help ensure that your furnace is ready to keep your home warm and comfortable during the cold months. Contact the Ignite Flame Services team today for further assistance.
